Learning Plan - REFLECTIONS

How accurate were your expectations of your learning needs and growth areas?

My expectations were mostly accurate. I knew I would need strong time-management and that juggling study around my children would be my biggest constraint, and that has definitely proven true. I also expected to feel overwhelmed at times, especially when learning unfamiliar technical concepts, and that has also aligned with reality. What I didn’t fully appreciate was how quickly my technical confidence would grow once I stayed consistent. My prediction that “a little bit every day” would work has been spot-on — the steady routine has helped me progress without the intense burnout I feared.

Were there any surprises / did you learn new things about yourself?

Yes, a few. I learned that I’m more resilient than I often think, especially when tackling new or uncomfortable topics. I also realised I can contribute meaningfully to group spaces even when I’m not the most technically experienced person in the room. Another surprise was how much I enjoy the creative side of coding; it confirmed that this career path genuinely suits me. I also discovered that I don’t shut down under pressure as much as I used to. Instead, I ask better questions, take breaks, and return with more clarity.

What challenges did you find in following your learning and wellbeing plan?

The biggest challenge has been predictability. With four children, my environment isn’t always quiet or uninterrupted, even with planning. There were days where my “little bit every day” motto was hard to achieve, especially when family needs escalated. Another challenge was sticking to my goal of speaking up more in group situations — I still catch myself wanting to take a backseat when I’m unsure. Balancing self-imposed expectations with real-life limits has been an ongoing adjustment.

What benefits did you experience from following your learning and wellbeing plan?

My plan helped me maintain consistency without burning out. Doing smaller daily chunks kept the overwhelm away and allowed the content to settle more naturally. Clear communication expectations also made it easier to work with others, because I wasn’t silently stressing or falling behind. Organising block-out times helped me protect family routines without sacrificing progress. Overall, the wellbeing plan gave me structure, confidence, and a sense of control and that made the whole experience more enjoyable and sustainable.
