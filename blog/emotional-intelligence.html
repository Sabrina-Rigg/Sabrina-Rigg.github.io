What is Emotional intelligence?


Emotional intelligence is the process of observing and identifying your own emotions and those of the people around you.
Although emotional intelligence is not merely knowing what emotions are, it is the act of remaining in control of one's emotional response in situations; especially those that are out of a person's control.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ and Iq are vastly different in that IQ is a measurable test for logical reasoning and problem solving “intelligence” whereas EQ refers to a person's understanding of their own emotions and those of others, while being able to manage emotional responses. More broadly, EQ is the building block to form strong bonds and relationships with others.

Why is EQ important?

Emotions are often seen as big technicolour outbursts of responses people have to situations that often feel out of our reach to control. By practicing and training the brain to improve emotional intelligence (practice mindfulness, journaling) we can better understand our triggers, responses and those of others, ultimately leading to a more happier and calmer life.
