Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.


Neuroplasticity suggests that our brain development and IQ is not just fixed. We are not only born to have a certain ‘smart’ like they taught at school. People have a choice in their intelligence because it is a practice, and our age does not hinder our ability in this practice. More so, learning new things and practicing them form stronger neuron pathways leading to a healthier brain with age

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.


I think the idea of neuroplasticity contributes to the growth mindset idea. Due to the brain constantly forming new neural pathways and gaining stronger bonds with practice, it gives us this notion of not being stuck in a situation we don’t like when it comes to learning or jobs or experience. We can learn something new at any age and flourish with every choice we make. For me, at 36, this gives me a lot of motivation and hunger to be a better person and to always strive towards success.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?


Ways to increase neuroplasticity are: 1- learning new skills 2 - practising mindfulness 3 - physical exercise is great for the brain 4 - proper nutrition 5 - get enough sleep

Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.


Growth mindset is the belief that our ability and intelligence are not just fixed, we have the ability to work towards better intelligence and abilities. It is relevant because, as stated above, it provides people with an option instead of being stuck in situations that are not ideal, we can improve ourselves and make our lives better.
