Te Whare Tapa Whā

Taha Tinana (physical Wellbeing)


I commit to exercise 3 times a week. This comes in the form of walking, running and the occasional youtube excerise video.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)


This is one aspect of life I feel I already have under control. Our four kids are our priority and we organise many adventures and family time with each other.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)


My journey with daily mindfulness practices and journaling are an immense help in this area. Also physical activity and reducing sugar intake are a massive impact on my mental health so I work on this everyday my making healthy choices.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)


This area of my life has always been a bit of a question mark. I have always been a spiritual person in regards to seeing nature and the universe as my guide through life, but I feel a calling to add more ritual to my practices and I think tying that in to mindfulness meditations everyday will really enrich my spirituality.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)


This of course ties in beautifully to Taha Wairua, my spirituality IS whenua. I practice grounding and being in nature whenever possible.
